A Star Among Us

She walks not on clouds, but the clouds still part,
For a girl who wore storms on her hopeful heart.
Life threw its punches — she laughed and took more,
Smiling through pain that most would abhor.

She’s chaos in slippers, a whirlwind in bloom,
The kind to throw pillows or dance through a room.
Random pinches, surprise attacks with delight —
A quirky madcap with warmth as her light.

She never had ease, but still she gave grace,
Lighting the dark with that radiant face.
Yet tides now are turning, the stars realign —
The world’s giving back what was always hers by design.

So raise up your glasses, let joy overflow,
For the sister who shines with an untamed glow.
A crown she denies, but deep down we see:
She’s magic and marvel — and weird royalty.
